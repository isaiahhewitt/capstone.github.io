Effects of COVID-19 on Gentrification in Rural Idaho Counties

What does gentrification mean?


Different areas of the country have different costs of living. The average person in a rural Idaho county likely makes significantly less money than the average person living in the Silicon Valley. Thus, it would make sense that someone living in a higher-income area will accrue much more money. The economic issue with gentrification arises when such a person transfers themself along with all their accrued wealth into a much poorer, smaller-scale economic system, essentially taking advantage of the people there by spending money, buying things like fancy houses and expensive cars, increasing economic market demand without actually supplying anything.

One famous example of gentrification is late-1990's New York City, in which economic booms encouraged rich people from central NYC to move to the eastern suburbs. Among several gentrifying neighborhoods, average rent cost increases more than doubled those of non-gentrifying neighborhoods. Gentrification creates an economic imbalance which enables more affluent people to support themselves off of the efforts of a poorer neighborhood without the more affluent people having to contribute as much in return. In order to understand gentrification, it's important to realize that money does not equate to material wealth; that the act of purchasing something money does not equate to doing the work to create the purchased object.
